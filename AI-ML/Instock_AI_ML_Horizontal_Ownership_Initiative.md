# Instock AI/ML Horizontal Ownership Initiative

## Context & Purpose

Instock operates in an environment where AI and machine learning capabilities are rapidly evolving across Chewy, yet we lack a structured mechanism to stay synchronized with these developments. Other horizontal ownership teams within Instock have established coordination points for operational, merchandising, and supply chain initiatives. Instock needs a similar interface for AI/ML—a dedicated connection point that allows us to monitor relevant AI initiatives, understand how they might apply to our workflows, and ensure our domain expertise informs broader AI development priorities.

The gap is not merely informational. Without a coordination mechanism, Instock risks missing opportunities to leverage new tools and patterns, duplicating effort that other teams have already solved, and operating in isolation from the AI/ML best practices that are emerging across Chewy. More critically, our absence from these conversations means that Instock-specific needs and constraints may not be adequately represented when strategic AI decisions are made. This initiative seeks to establish that connection point, creating a lightweight but effective engagement model that positions Instock as both a consumer and contributor within Chewy's AI ecosystem.

## Proposed Engagement Strategy

We propose four low-effort initiatives that can be implemented immediately. These are designed as low-hanging fruit—quick to stand up, minimal resource commitment, and structured so we can iterate based on what proves most valuable. The approach follows best practices we've seen work elsewhere: operate as both a hub (providing common building blocks and centralized resources) and a coach (enabling coordinated collaboration across business units). The options aren't mutually exclusive and can be combined:

1. **Resource Billboard**: A centralized location (SharePoint or Confluence) that aggregates AI/ML documentation, links to relevant tools and projects, and serves as a single source of truth. This creates a persistent, searchable repository that addresses the visibility gap without requiring active monitoring.

2. **Recurring Follow-ups**: A predictable cadence for information exchange—format TBD, but could be regular meetings with AI/ML stakeholders, newsletter-style updates, or a hybrid. The key is creating rhythm that keeps Instock current without ad-hoc coordination for every update.

3. **Open Office Hours**: Optional drop-in sessions where Instock team members can bring questions, explore AI applications to their workflows, or simply learn what's available. This low-pressure environment helps surface use cases that might not emerge through formal channels.

4. **Share-Back Sessions**: Instock-led sessions to share our AI/ML experiences and learnings with the broader organization, or tagging into sessions led by other teams. This positions us as contributors to the AI ecosystem, not just consumers, ensuring our domain expertise informs broader strategy.

These range from passive (Resource Billboard) to active (Share-Back Sessions), allowing us to calibrate engagement intensity based on strategic priority and resource constraints.

## Known AI/ML Projects in Instock Ecosystem

Instock already has AI/ML work in progress, but it's happening in a decentralized way. The Predictive Out-of-Stock (OOS) project—either Instock-originated or heavily overlapping with our workflows—and the Email CPFR Debriefing Tool both operate within our domain. The coordination mechanism would provide a framework to ensure these projects are visible, learnings are shared, and opportunities for cross-pollination are identified, with variable levels of project engagement or management depending on what makes sense for each initiative.

## Open Questions for Direction Setting

Several strategic questions need to be addressed to structure this effectively:

1. **Primary value-add focus**: What should be the strategic emphasis? Maximizing visibility to AI initiatives across Chewy so we can identify and adopt relevant capabilities quickly? Prioritizing share-back of AI best practices and Instock-specific learnings to position us as thought leaders? Or creating a backlog of AI opportunities for the Instock team to translate awareness into actionable project priorities? There may be other strategic priorities that should take precedence.

2. **Engagement cadence and format**: What frequency makes sense—weekly, monthly, quarterly? Should the format be meetings, written updates, or a hybrid? This will depend on both strategic priority level and practical bandwidth constraints.

3. **Resource allocation**: Any constraints or priorities to consider? Should we start with one option and expand, or implement multiple simultaneously? Understanding resource boundaries will help us scope the initial implementation appropriately.

The goal is to establish momentum and create a foundation that evolves based on what proves most valuable for Instock and Chewy's broader AI strategy.
